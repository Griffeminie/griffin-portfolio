Seminars & Workshops

Serverlesspresso Workshop

AWS Student Community Day — Southfest · Feb 16, 2026 · 1:00–4:00 PM

Philippine Christian University, Aguinaldo Hwy, Dasmariñas, Cavite

Hosted by Marco Dalanon as part of an IT field trip. Serverlesspresso is a hands-on workshop that introduces event-driven, serverless architecture through a "pop-up coffee shop" scenario: participants build and deploy a serverless backend ordering system with components for customers, baristas, and display screens, using AWS Lambda, EventBridge, API Gateway, and Step Functions to see how serverless apps scale without managing infrastructure directly.

Session Notes — From Cloud Theory to Cloud Deploys

  • Cloud computing fundamentals, mental models, and delivery approaches
  • AWS infrastructure: ALB, ECS, RDS, ECR, Cognito, DNS lookups
  • Global infrastructure: regions, availability zones, edge locations (POPs), latency
  • Network infrastructure: VPCs (regional scale) and subnets (AZ scale)
  • Public vs. private subnets and security groups
  • Build order: network → data layer → compute → load balancing → DNS
  • Relational vs. non-relational databases — RDS vs. DynamoDB (and using both)
  • Docker fundamentals and ECS architecture: clusters, services, tasks, task definitions
  • EC2 vs. Fargate
  • Horizontal vs. vertical scaling, and the load balancer "security and routing stack"
  • Routing traffic with Route 53

Atlassian Lunch & Learn: Unleash Collective Knowledge with Rovo AI

Atlassian & ServiceRocket · Feb 27, 2026 · 2:00–6:00 PM

The Yard, Menarco Tower, 32nd Street, BGC, Taguig City

Attended as part of an IT field trip, with sessions led by Belinda Tee (Director of Solution Architecture, ServiceRocket) and Ian Ragudo (Developer Advocate, Atlassian). The seminar traced the evolution of teamwork from co-located collaboration to digital and now AI-augmented collaboration, framed through Atlassian's System of Work: aligning work to the right goals, planning and tracking work together, and harnessing collective knowledge. A key theme was making AI part of the team itself — contrasting "tool thinking" (AI used only as a utility, which under-utilizes it) with "teammate thinking" (AI embedded as a collaborator within the system of work), and why this shift only works when AI operates as a platform rather than a bolt-on tool. A live demo showed a day working alongside an AI teammate, including streamlined workflow automation in Jira and automatic generation of release notes.

Session Notes — Rovo AI & the Atlassian System of Work

  • The evolution of teamwork: co-located → digital collaboration → AI-augmented teamwork
  • Atlassian's System of Work: how modern work is designed
  • Aligning work to the right goals
  • Planning and tracking work together
  • Harnessing collective knowledge
  • Making AI part of the team: AI as a teammate within the system of work
  • Tool thinking vs. teammate thinking — why tool thinking under-utilizes AI
  • Why this approach only works as a platform (leadership lens)
  • "A day with your AI teammate" — maximizing collective knowledge through human-AI collaboration
  • Live demo: streamlined workflow automation in Jira, including auto-generated release notes
